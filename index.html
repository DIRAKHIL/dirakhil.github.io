LOG LINE

1990s, Andhra Pradesh. When a father's suicide leaves a boy alone, he learns that only money and power command respect. As he fulfils the promise he made to his father, he rises with a childhood friend, turns his anger into crime, and builds an empire through fear. Together, they become a fearsome MP and MLA, ruling with ruthless hands.

Their trusted right-hand man, a head constable, quietly bends the law for them — until his daughter, a sincere young police officer and the quiet strength he lived for, is framed, broken, and driven to suicide by their cruelty.

Shattered by her loss, from this moment, the high-voltage, mass-loaded action drama blast begins:

STORY BEATS

• Father's suicide leaves a young boy alone and angry in 1980s Andhra Pradesh.

• He rises with a childhood friend, turning to crime and building power.

• They become feared MLA and MP, ruling the region through violence and control.

• A head constable, loyal but morally torn, becomes their right-hand man.

• His daughter fulfills his dream, becoming a superior police officer at his own station.

• She unknowingly arrests the MP's son, causing tension with her father.

• The political family frames her in a false case and drives her to suicide.

• Shattered by her death, the head constable breaks his silence.

• Fear is broken—with a final, the High-voltage, Mass-loaded Action Sequence.
